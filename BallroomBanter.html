Ballroom Banter
Card Mechanics
The most important mechanic of the game are the cards themselves. I worked on developing the mechanics to store and display the player's deck and hand, as well as tracking cards that were played and discarded. I also created each card's effect, and a system to evaluate the outcome of advanced card combinations.
Game Hub and Shop
Outside of the card battles themselves, the player can interact with the characters as well as view their deck and upgrade/remove cards. I was responsible for making sure these smaller connection systems worked correctly and managed the player's data.
Data Saving and Analytics
I built a robust save system for Ballroom Banter to ensure that game data never gets lost. All important data is put into a custom class and then serialized and saved on the device, which occurs on application closing as well as certain checkpoints in the game. I also implemented devtodev analytics into the game, allowing analysis of player gameplay, session length, etc.
